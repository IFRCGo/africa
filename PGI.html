Latest News Africa Region

Protection Gender & Inclusion during COVID-19:The COVID-19 Pandemic has affected people differently based on their sex, gender and other factors, including age, disability, sexual orientation, health status, legal status, ethnicity, and other aspects of the person. The pandemic has intensified existing gender inequalities, increased incidences of sexual and gender-based violence (SGBV) and violence against children across Africa due to the lock down and harsh economic times. Protection, Gender and Inclusion has been mainstreamed in the different covid -19 responses to address violence, discrimination or exclusion, in all contexts where Africa National societies work.

SGBV Prevention and Messaging :
Kenya Red Cross are integrating SGBV Prevention and Response messaging during COVID-19 awareness sessions to community's food, non-food items and WASH items distribution. They are also supporting GBV Coordination mechanisms at the county level. Community Health Volunteers have also received training on SGBV in emergencies. Prepositioning and distribution of drugs and commodities to respond to SGBV.They have activated their Community Protection Watch Groups for response on SGBV at community level and Integration of PSS 24hrs services through toll free tele counselling services- 1199.
Mozambique Red Cross : Mozambique Red Cross : together with IFRC are ensuring that in dissemination of COVID 19 messages, SGBV information and referral pathways are shared as well. Reactivation of community protection structures is also being enhanced. Mozambique government structures at community level have been supported by the Red Cross through training and coordination on SGBV prevention and response.
Togolese Red Cross : through `Papa Champions` and Mothers club are sharing messages on SGBV including referral pathways to communities through community awareness sessions, songs, radio programs.

Inclusion of persons with disabilities :
Gambia Red Cross society have been working closely with the Gambia organization of the disabled to ensure that persons with disabilities are not left behind in the Covid -19 response. To date 75 PWD households have received food and cash relief from Gambia Red Cross while differently abled groups have also managed to access hand washing facilities distribute by GRC 20 participants from the Gambia Association of the deaf and Dumb were taken through a one-day training on Covid-19 prevention and response. This training was carried out to help sensitize the deaf and dumb community who have been left out in the sensitization forums on Covid-19. The training was in partnership with the department of health.

    Dignity Access Participation Safety (DAPS)

    The DAPS Framework provides a simple but comprehensive guide for addressing the core actions in Red Cross Red Crescent emergency programming.

    Dignity

    For the Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, human dignity means respect for the life and integrity of individuals. All Red Cross and Red Crescent emergency responders and emergency response programmes should contribute to the maintenance and promotion of human dignity.

    Access

    Emergency response programmes should provide access for all individuals and sub-groups within the affected population. Accordingly, the beneficiary selection and prioritisation criteria for accessing humanitarian goods, services and protection must be informed by a gender and diversity analysis to ensure that the assistance and protection reach people who are most at risk.

    Participation

    Participation refers to the full, equal and meaningful involvement of all members of the community in decision-making processes and activities that affect their lives. Sharing of information is a Core Humanitarian Standard for accountability to beneficiaries and is critical part of participation.

    Safety

    Females, males and other gender identities of all ages and backgrounds within affected communities have different needs regarding their physical safety. Monitoring the safety of project sites from the perspectives of diverse groups is essential to ensure that the assistance provided meets everyone’s needs and concerns in an equitable manner.

    The International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (IFRC) is the world's largest humanitarian organization, with 190 member National Societies. As part of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, our work is guided by seven fundamental principles; humanity, impartiality, neutrality, independence, voluntary service, unity and universality.
