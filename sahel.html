The Sahel+ is a network of ten national Societies coordinating on matters of mutual interest. The network comprises the national Societies of Burkina Faso, Cape Verde, Chad, the Gambia, Guinea Bissau, Guinea Conakry, Mali, Mauritania, Niger and Senegal. The network also includes the IFRC, the ICRC, and partner national Societies.
The 2019 Sahel+ General Assembly established a Resilience Platform with 3 technical groups (shelter, food security and migration), with the objective of promoting coordination, partnership and cooperation within the RCRC Movement in the Sahel and with external partners.

The migration technical group (originally constituted in 2017 as a Sahel+ migration network), brings together the migration focal points of the different Sahel+ members. The group aims to:

  • • Establish a forum for cooperation and information sharing in order to inform migration-related initiatives in the Sahel region;
  • • Developing and reinforcing the response capacities of the RCRC Movement in the Sahel when it comes to responding to migrant’s humanitarian needs and social inclusion challenges;
  • • Developing advocacy initiatives and integrated programmes to improve the conditions of migrants and displaced persons in the Sahel region;
  • • Engaging a dialogue with governments, regional platforms, external partners and academic institutions.

    • With support from the AMiRA programme
    The AMiRA programme (Action for Migrants: route-Based Assistance), funded by DFID, aims to assist vulnerable transit migrants, returnees and vulnerable host communities. In the Sahel region, the programme is implemented by the British Red Cross along with the IFRC, four host National Societies (Burkina Faso, guinea Conakry, Mali and Niger), and two partner National Societies (Danish Red Cross and Spanish Red Cross), operating along the main migration routes in the region. Within the framework of this programme, the IFRC aims at reinforcing the capacities, strategic vision and the positioning of concerned National Societies, including through humanitarian diplomacy initiatives and the establishment of partnerships with other migration actors and stakeholders.

    The International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (IFRC) is the world's largest humanitarian organization, with 190 member National Societies. As part of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, our work is guided by seven fundamental principles; humanity, impartiality, neutrality, independence, voluntary service, unity and universality.
