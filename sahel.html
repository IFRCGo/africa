AMiRA Programme

The AMiRA programme (Action for Migrants: route-Based Assistance), funded by DFID, aims to assist vulnerable transit migrants, returnees and vulnerable host communities.

In the Sahel region, the programme is implemented by the British Red Cross along with the IFRC, four host National Societies (Burkina Faso, guinea Conakry, Mali and Niger), and two partner National Societies (Danish Red Cross and Spanish Red Cross), operating along the main migration routes in the region.

Within the framework of this programme, the IFRC aims at reinforcing the capacities, strategic vision and the positioning of concerned National Societies, including through humanitarian diplomacy initiatives and the establishment of partnerships with other migration actors and stakeholders.

Resilience Platform

The 2019 Sahel+ General Assembly established a Resilience Platform with 3 technical groups (shelter, food security and migration), with the objective of promoting coordination, partnership and cooperation within the RCRC Movement in the Sahel and with external partners.
