Novel Corona Disease

The outbreak of COVID-19, a novel coronavirus identified in late 2019, was declared a public health emergency of international concern by WHO on 30 January. IFRC is coordinating with all National Societies – especially those with active cases – to support with monitoring, issuing advisories, supporting the pre-positioning of personal protective equipment for high-risk activities, scenario planning and analysing risk, and other actions.
For more information on COVID-19 Operation visit go.ifrc.org

Africa National Society Covid-19 Update

Angola

The national society leadership has communicated that they hired an SG finally. The President shared a request letter for support during Covid-19 which our Health delegate is following up.
IFRC cluster office has linked the NS to Nestle and a few other Private Sector .
Angola continues to be a challenge as the NS is just building a new team.

Botswana

IFRC team supported the NS in unpacking the NS auxiliary role in a context of middle-income country. Visibility, communication, and Partnership management and domestic RM are critical in this context. They have since engaged better with the government in the preparation of Covid-19. As a result their NS DM coordinator has been appointed to the National Emergency Operating Centre (NEOC) for COVID-19 response by the government.
The NS has trained volunteers and staff who have been conducting health promotion, hand washing demonstration in relation to Covid-19. NS has also been given the task to support in PSS activities of people in quarantine

Lesotho

The NS has trained its personnel both volunteers and staff in readiness for Covid-19 and briefed and sensitised its Governance team on Covid-19 preparedness activities. The NS has been selected to receive the capacity building fund from IFRC. Lesotho is the only country that has not registered a COVID case.

Malawi

The NS Trained the response teams (partners, staff and volunteers) on COVID- 19 stigma prevention, on Psychosocial Support (PSS) and Safer Access. The trained team has been doing door to door community sensitisation. The NS has become the partner of choice for government and non-movement partners like the UN and GIZ. Great progress is being made in the COVID response

Mozambique

The NS has the acting SG still running the affairs of the NS. The IFRC head of country office has ended his mission. The incoming HoC will resume as soon as travel restrictions are lifted.
NS is linking up well with the government on COVID response. Funding remains a challenge in this area

Namibia

The NS has continued to improve in its operations, and it is growing in confidence. The NS has been invited by the government to serves on the National Risk Management Committee on National and Regional levels respectively. The NS has an opportunity to influence major decisions and key messages regarding covid-19 and possibly beyond. IFRC is working very closely with the NS to continue to position them as the leading humanitarian agency in Namibia.

South Africa

The NS has been very proactive since the covid-19 began to hit the country and have been entrusted by the government to assist in contact tracing and testing as well as giving sensitisation to the communities and the public on keeping distance, sanitising , handwashing and also supporting with food to the homeless. They have received some negative publicity through an audio message that was shared insinuating that they are spreading the Covid-19. The government has come out publicly to support the NS and to continue to work with the NS.The IFRC cluster head also met with the Minister of health to continue to profile the work of the RC in South Africa. IFRC has facilitated an MoU between the Ministry of Health and the South Africa Red Cross which includes yearly subventions. Great Movement response in South Africa led by IFRC
Governance and Management of SARCS is now stabilizing.

Swaziland/Eswatin

The NS has been selected to be part of the National Emergency Task Force on Covid-19 by the government and the government personnel helped in the training of Staff and volunteer on COVID-19 preparedness & response and safer access. They have since been deployed to conduct dissemination of information to communities on coronavirus. With the help of the IFRC cluster they have produced Information Education and Communication (IEC) material in local language. They are collaborating with the media to sensitise the public and reduce misinformation. Through the support of government, the NSs run clinic provided medical supplies to COVID-19 affected people. In preparedness the NSs in collaboration with the government have procured and distribution of PPE, shelter material for 4 isolation tents) and Hand washing equipment.

Zambia

This NS has requested the Netherlands Red Cross to help pay off the cases settled out of court with former staff in a loan model which the partner has agreed to. Though with a weak leadership, the NS has managed to improve on its operations. With the help of PNS in country, ICRC and IFRC they have trained volunteers and staff on Covid-19. The government has provided posters and the volunteers have been distributing. Plans are under way to support risk communication and community engagement activities. The NS has gotten quotation from the one insurance company that responded to their request on covering volunteers during this Covid-19. Negotiations are still on going.

Zimbabwe

Some of the in-country PNSs have left the country but they have offered support for Covid-19. Staff and volunteers at the Red Cross Clinic have been actively involved in the screening and referral of suspected Covid-19 cases. There is a very big opportunity for RC clinic to augment the health services of the country as a screening and testing point. Officially designated role in RCCE pillar, ZRCS is coordinating with relevant authorities, undertaking screening at the ZRCS clinic and food distribution processes, providing health services for Non-Covid-19 conditions (most hospitals have closed or suspended non-emergency service).
Investigation by the OIA&I is still ongoing although the country visit will take place once travel restrictions have been lifted. Cluster office is still facing challenges with the NS DREF returns. Email sent to the regional director for advice on next steps.
